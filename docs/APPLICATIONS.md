# Applications

pyGDIS is designed for nonlinear systems in which trajectories are observed over a control-parameter sweep or an ordered progression.

## Nonlinear benchmark systems

- Lorenz, Rössler, and Chen attractors
- Logistic and other discrete maps
- Duffing and Van der Pol oscillators
- Chua circuits

## Engineering systems

- Phase-locked loops
- Power-system transient and voltage stability
- Converter and microgrid stability
- Mechanical vibration and structural health monitoring
- Control-system instability and loss of synchronization

## Physical and environmental systems

- Fluid and plasma dynamics
- Climate-state transitions
- Ecological regime shifts
- Chemical reaction networks

## Biological systems

- Transcriptomic trajectories
- Cell-state transitions and differentiation
- Cancer progression and treatment response
- Perturbation-response dynamics

## Appropriate use

GDIS is most informative when trajectories are comparable, sufficiently sampled, and ordered by a meaningful control variable or progression variable. The score should be validated against domain-specific references whenever possible.
